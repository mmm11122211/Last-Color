# Last Color

Last Color (originally developed as Teleporter 2) is a stylized, single-player action RPG built for SI 342: Games & UX at the University of Michigan. The game explores a high-contrast world where players must fight to restore color to a landscape being drained into grayscale by a mysterious entity.

🎮 The Concept

In a world defined by vivid vitality, a grayscale villain has begun sucking the color out of the environment. As her influence spreads, the space destabilizes and fades into a lifeless monochrome. Players take on the role of the Hero, a lone guardian whose primary function is to protect the realm and defeat the source of decay.

🚀 Gameplay & Mechanics

The game centers on a focused combat loop within enclosed, color-decaying arenas.

Projectile-Based Combat: Players use a grenade-throwing mechanic that emphasizes timing, positioning, and spatial awareness rather than traditional melee.

Limited Resources: Rely on a finite number of explosive grenades. Players must collect timed orb pickups in the arena to replenish ammo.

Environmental Feedback (Color Decay): The world itself acts as a health bar. As the Boss drains color or the player takes damage, the environment turns grayscale more rapidly.

Instant Failure Condition: The Boss actively tracks the player's position. Direct contact results in an immediate game over, requiring high spatial awareness.

Difficulty Scaling: Progression spans 5 levels of escalating difficulty, featuring faster enemies and more aggressive attack patterns.

🎨 Visual Identity & UX

The project's "Clash of Aesthetics" is central to its identity, contrasting vibrant neon-digital styles with dark gothic elements.

The Palette: A signature blend of Hot Pink (#BC2663), Deep Purple (#1A1426), and Absolute Black (#000000) for the Hero's world, set against a monotone Grayscale Boss.

Shape Language: Adheres to a "Pointed Geometry" philosophy—using sharp angles and distinct edges to create tension and urgency, while avoiding "friendly" soft/organic curves.

Typography: Features the Griffy font for a whimsical, old-age vibe that highlights the theme of "creativity prevailing."

Interactive Clarity: Bright yellow (#FFB804) is used for UI elements like health bars and trajectory indicators to ensure clear visibility against complex backgrounds.

🧪 User Research & Testing

Developed through a human-centered design process, the game underwent concept testing with 20 participants from the University of Michigan community.

Testing Goals: Evaluated the appeal of the "Color Decay" mechanic and the clarity of the HUD/Control systems.

Findings: Research confirmed that high-contrast visuals improved threat detection by up to 42% in similar combat scenarios, leading to our final aesthetic choice.
